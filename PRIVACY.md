# ClearPath Privacy Policy

**Plain English version — because our users deserve clear communication.**

---

## The Short Version

ClearPath collects absolutely nothing about you. It has no servers. Your data never leaves your browser. Full stop.

---

## What Data ClearPath Collects

**None.**

ClearPath does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data, browsing history, usage data, or any other information about you or how you use the extension.

---

## What Stays on Your Device

Your settings (font size, color theme, TTS voice, etc.) are saved using your browser's built-in `storage.sync` API. This syncs your settings across your own devices via your browser account (e.g., your Google account for Chrome). ClearPath never sees or touches this data — it goes directly between your browser and your browser account.

---

## The LLM Simplification Feature

If you use the **Simplify** or **Summarize** features, selected text is sent to your chosen LLM API provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, or a local Ollama instance):

- The text goes **directly from your browser to the API provider**
- It does **not** pass through any ClearPath server — there is no ClearPath server
- It is subject to **your API provider's privacy policy**, not ours
- If you use **Ollama** (local mode), the text never leaves your device at all

You can use ClearPath without ever using the LLM features. Read Aloud, Reading Mode, and all other features work entirely offline.

---

## Analytics & Tracking

None. Zero. ClearPath contains no analytics code, no tracking pixels, no telemetry, no crash reporting that sends data anywhere. Nothing.

---

## Third-Party Services

ClearPath does not integrate with any third-party services other than the optional LLM API you configure yourself.

---

## Open Source

ClearPath is completely open source. Every line of code is publicly auditable at [github.com/clearpath-ext/clearpath-extension](https://github.com/clearpath-ext/clearpath-extension). You don't have to take our word for any of this — you can read the code.
